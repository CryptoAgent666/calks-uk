## Content Depth Analysis

### Word Count Distribution (Calculator Pages)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total calculator pages | 331 |
| Minimum | 128 words (probate-fee-calculator) |
| Maximum | 359 words (vat-flat-rate-calculator) |
| Average | 195 words |
| Median | 184 words |
| Pages under 200 words | 218 (66%) |
| Pages over 300 words | ~40 (12%) |

**Assessment:** Calculator pages are tool pages (similar to product pages where 300+ words is the floor). Currently, most pages contain only: title, description, calculator widget, disclaimer, and FAQ (if present). The interactive calculator itself provides substantial value, but the surrounding text content is thin by traditional standards.

**Important context:** For tool/calculator pages, Google evaluates the utility of the interactive element, not just word count. The React calculators provide genuine first-hand utility. However, adding contextual content (how-to guides, worked examples, related legislation summaries) would strengthen both E-E-A-T and keyword coverage.

## Critical Recommendations

### Priority 1 — Expand Calculator Page Content (High Impact)

**Problem:** 218 of 331 calculators have under 200 words of text content.

**Solution:** Add a "How It Works" section to each calculator page with:
- 2-3 paragraph explanation of the calculation methodology
- A worked example with specific numbers
- Key rates/thresholds in a summary table
- Link to the official HMRC/GOV.UK source

**Target:** 400+ words per calculator page (excluding interactive widget).

**Implementation:** Create a `how-it-works` content field in `calculators.ts` or a separate data file with per-calculator explanatory content.

### Priority 2 — Expand Service Pages (Medium Impact)

**Problem:** All 6 service pages are under the 800-word guideline.

| Page | Current | Target | Action |
|------|---------|--------|--------|
| /about/ | 297 | 800+ | Add team background, UK finance expertise, founding story, technology approach details |
| /methodology/ | 629 | 800+ | Add worked example of verification process, screenshots of source checks |
| /privacy/ | 358 | 800+ | Expand cookie categories, add data retention schedule, accessibility statement |
| /terms/ | 442 | 800+ | Add examples for key terms, expand disclaimer section, add YMYL-specific language |
| /contact/ | 224 | 600+ | Add office hours, response SLA details, social media, FAQ about contacting |
| /updates/ | 259 | 600+ | Will grow naturally as updates are added |

### Priority 3 — Add "Last Updated" Date to Calculators (Low Effort, High Signal)

Add a visible "Last updated: April 2026" date to calculator pages, especially tax/finance calculators. This is a strong freshness and trust signal for both users and search engines.

### Priority 4 — Inline Source Citations (Medium Impact)

Add hyperlinked citations on calculator pages:
- "Rates from [HMRC Income Tax rates 2025/26](https://www.gov.uk/income-tax-rates)"
- "Based on [NI rates and thresholds](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/rates-and-thresholds-for-employers-2025-to-2026)"

This strengthens both E-E-A-T expertise and AI citation readiness.

### Priority 5 — Expand FAQ Coverage (Medium Impact)

Currently 37/331 calculators have FAQs. Expand to at least the top 100 most-searched calculator types. Each FAQ should have 3-5 questions with specific, data-rich answers.
